# Census (Fivetran Activations) — Competitor Reference

## Overview
Census was a reverse ETL and data activation platform founded in 2018. In 2024, Census was acquired by Fivetran, and its product has been rebranded/integrated as **Fivetran Activations** — the reverse ETL and data activation arm of Fivetran's data movement platform. Fivetran is headquartered in Oakland, CA and is one of the leading data integration (ELT) platforms.

## Positioning (Post-Acquisition)
Fivetran positions Activations (Census) as the reverse ETL complement to its ELT ingestion business — completing a bidirectional data movement story: "Move data in with Fivetran ELT, activate it outward with Fivetran Activations." Targets data-mature organizations already in the Fivetran ecosystem.

## Core Product Areas

### Reverse ETL
- Sync data models/queries from data warehouses (BigQuery, Snowflake, Databricks, Redshift, PostgreSQL) to business tools (CRMs, MAPs, ad platforms, support tools).

### Audience Hub
- No-code visual audience and segment builder on top of warehouse data. Enables marketers to self-serve without SQL knowledge.

### Syncs
- Configurable, scheduled or event-triggered data pipelines from warehouse to destinations. Supports upsert, mirror, and append modes.

### Channels / Destinations
- Not a channel platform — activates data INTO other tools. Destinations include: Salesforce, HubSpot, Braze, Klaviyo, Iterable, Marketo, Facebook Ads, Google Ads, Zendesk, Intercom, Slack, and 200+ others.

### Developer Resources
- REST API for managing syncs programmatically. Git-based version control (similar to Hightouch).

## Relationship to Hightouch
Census/Fivetran Activations and Hightouch are the two main reverse ETL competitors. Post-acquisition, Census now benefits from Fivetran's enterprise distribution, but Hightouch has moved further upmarket with AI Decisioning and Journeys. They are in direct competition.

## Strengths
- Fivetran's enterprise distribution and existing customer relationships post-acquisition
- Strong no-code audience builder (Audience Hub) for marketers
- Bidirectional data movement narrative (ELT + reverse ETL) is compelling for Fivetran customers
- 200+ destination connectors

## Weaknesses
- Acquisition integration uncertainty may affect product velocity
- Less innovative product expansion than Hightouch (no AI Decisioning equivalent yet)
- Not a standalone engagement platform — needs a MAP/engagement tool alongside
- Brand recognition transitioning from "Census" to "Fivetran Activations"

## Pricing Model
Usage-based (sync volume, number of destinations). Bundled with Fivetran contracts for existing customers. Custom enterprise pricing.

## Key Differentiators vs. Insider One
- Fivetran Activations is a data pipeline tool, not an engagement platform
- Companies using Census/Fivetran Activations still need a separate tool for customer communication
- Insider One's CDP and engagement capabilities are tightly integrated — no separate activation layer needed
- Insider wins with marketing teams that want a single platform; Census wins with data engineering teams that want control

## Recommended Talking Points (Insider vs. Census/Fivetran Activations)
- "Fivetran Activations is an infrastructure tool for your data team — Insider is the marketing platform that powers customer experiences"
- "Insider doesn't require a reverse ETL layer to activate your data — the CDP and engagement are one integrated system"
- "With Insider, marketers can self-serve personalization without waiting for a data engineering sync cycle"
